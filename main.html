STCAST was founded in 2018 under the administration of former Mayor Daniel Batosalem Jr. The establishment of the local college was perceived to be a hostile takeover in a bid to cease the operation and eventually replace the extension campus of the University of Southeastern Philippines (USEP). But the establishment and operation of STCAST that time was marred with irregularities. Whats more appalling was that the national government did not yet subsidize its operation like state-owned Universities and Colleges (SUCs). Tuition refunds for USEP coeds, delayed salaries of college teachers, and the flawed STCAST ordinance was among the issues and concerns that highlighted the major mess in STCAST overall operations. All these and more prompted the Evangelista administration to take a decisive step to solve the problems in the LGU-owned STCAST since the issues were lingering and were left unresolved during the Batosalem administration.

The Santo Tomas College of Agriculture, Sciences, and Technology (STCAST), located in Santo Tomas, Davao del Norte, is a higher education institution recognized by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED). It offers various programs, including Bachelor of Public Administration, Bachelor of Science in Office Administration, Bachelor of Technical-Vocational Teacher Education, and Bachelor of Science in Agribusiness. The college has achieved institutional recognition and compliance with CHED standards, ensuring quality education for its students. Under the leadership of its president, Dr. Diobein C. Flores, STCAST has seen significant growth, including infrastructure development, securing subsidies, and implementing a free education program under Republic Act 10931. The college is dedicated to advancing education in agriculture, sciences, and technology, emphasizing innovation and excellence.
